# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Senior engineering and staff-level hiring managers reading this PDF on a modern screen. They are skim-first: 5–10 seconds of scanning to decide whether to read closely. They compare many resumes in a sitting and pattern-match aggressively. Context is high-signal, low-attention — the document needs to win in the scan before any bullet gets read.

## Product Purpose

A personal-marketing artifact that positions Andrew Solomon as a **Senior Salesforce Engineer / Technical Lead who is also a modern web and AI craftsman** — a deliberate bridge identity. Success is the reader, in their first 5 seconds, registering both halves of that identity (deep Salesforce platform credibility *and* current taste in web/AI tooling) and treating the rest of the resume as proof rather than introduction.

## Brand Personality

**Precise. Modern. Confident.** Reads like a well-engineered product page from Stripe or Vercel: every glyph and rule earns its place, the typographic system is the design, and confidence comes from restraint rather than ornament. Voice is direct and results-forward — no jargon padding, no hedging.

## Anti-references

Every choice should be explicitly not from any of these gravity wells:

- **Generic Word / LinkedIn resume templates.** Times New Roman, blue hyperlink underlines, two-column corporate, mid-document horizontal rules every section. The default everyone has.
- **Salesforce-corporate aesthetic.** Cloud-blue gradients, icon-per-skill, Trailhead-mascot warmth, Salesforce-blue accent color. Signal Salesforce depth without inheriting its visual register.
- **Designer-portfolio cosplay.** HUGE display type, full-bleed photos, asymmetric manifesto layouts, "creative director auditioning" energy. This is an engineer's resume, not a portfolio reel.
- **AI-slop SaaS landing page.** Gradient text, glassmorphism, identical card grids, hero-metric templates, generative-feeling hero illustrations.

The current `main.tex` already trips one of these (the bright Salesforce-blue accent at `RGB 0,150,255`) — that is part of what dark-mode work should resolve.

## Design Principles

1. **Bridge, don't split.** The resume must read as Salesforce-deep AND modern-web/AI-current in a single visual register. No section should feel like it belongs to a different document than another section.
2. **Skim-first hierarchy.** Front-load identity, role, and bridge signal before anyone reads a bullet. The first second pays for the next ten.
3. **Show, don't perform.** Design quality is evidence of taste, not a thesis statement. Restraint reads as confidence; ornament reads as insecurity.
4. **Refuse adjacent templates.** When a choice could be lifted from Word, Trailhead, a Behance portfolio, or a v0 landing page, choose the alternative that isn't.
5. **Earn every mark.** Every rule, color, and weight change must do load-bearing work. Decorative elements that survive a "what does this communicate?" challenge are the only ones that stay.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No special constraints beyond standard PDF rendering on modern screens. Treat as a license — real typographic craft, deliberate color, and asymmetric rhythm are on the table without ATS-readability or print-B&W dictating layout. Body contrast should still meet WCAG AA on the chosen background, but that is the only hard floor.
